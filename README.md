# capstone-project-mbah-# Dano Airlines Customer Satisfaction Report

## Overview

Dano Airlines recently recorded a customer satisfaction rate of **43%**, its lowest level on record. This assessment is based on feedback from **130,000+ passengers** and highlights key drivers of dissatisfaction, emerging patterns across passenger segments, and areas requiring urgent improvement.

This README provides a structured summary suitable for use in a GitHub project documenting the analysis.

---

## Key Insights

### **1. Overall Satisfaction**

* Only **43%** of passengers reported being satisfied with their experience.
* This marks the airline’s lowest satisfaction performance to date.

---

### **2. Satisfaction by Class of Travel**

* **Business Class:** 69% satisfaction — highest among all passenger groups.
* **Economy & Premium Economy:** Significantly lower satisfaction (exact figures to be analyzed further).

*Insight:* Higher-paying customers are considerably more satisfied, indicating service consistency issues across cabins.

---

### **3. Satisfaction by Customer Type**

* **Returning Customers:** 48% satisfaction
* **First‑time Customers:** 24% satisfaction

*Insight:* A poor first‑time experience is a major contributor to low overall satisfaction.

---

### **4. Satisfaction by Travel Type**

* **Business Travel:** 60% satisfaction
* **Personal/Leisure Travel:** 30% satisfaction

*Insight:* Leisure travelers are having significantly poorer experiences, likely impacting brand reputation and repeat bookings.

---

### **5. Operational Metrics**

* **Average Departure Delay:** 14.7 minutes
* **Average Arrival Delay:** 15.1 minutes

*Insight:* Operational delays appear to be contributing factors to negative passenger perceptions.

---

## Recommended Areas of Focus

Based on the insights above:

* Improve **economy cabin experience**, especially comfort and service quality.
* Enhance **first‑time customer onboarding**, communication, and support.
* Reduce delays through stronger **operational scheduling and turnaround processes**.
* Address gaps in the **leisure travel experience** (e.g., value perception, onboard comfort).
